PUBLIC SPACE





Question: How much of the earth's freshwater is in ice sheets and glaciers?



Idea #1: This would be a series of posters put up around bus stations. The graphic on them represents the fact
that glaciers hold a very large percentage of the earth's freshwater, but due to Global Warming, they are melting
at a record rate. The sea level would rise by over 200 feet is all the glaciers were to melt today, which would almost
equal the current height of glaciers. The QR code links to a mobile website with fast facts about the situation.



Idea #2: This would be a sculpture situated in a public space, such as Boston's Seaport using the same form as in
the posters. However, the sea levels would be represented by a piece of wood, and the glaciers by a carved piece of
ice which would melt over time, giving a more visual demonstration of the issue.



Idea #3: This would involve filling up an ice rink with a series of small 3D models in the same shape as the original
graphic. The models would have the facts written on them, and as visitors take them, the ice beneath will be revealed,
as if it has melted away.



Reflection: It was said in critique, and I agree, that Idea #1 is the strongest as it is simple, ubiquitous, and placed in an
area frequented by many types of people.
